Concentration camp


Concentration camps were made by the Nazis,
they were small fenced areas where they would use them for forced labor
and then execute them using gas chambers.

Purpose of Concentration camps:

To incarcerate people whom the Nazi regime perceived to be a security threat.
These people were incarcerated for indefinite amounts of time.
To eliminate individuals and small, targeted groups of individuals by murder,
away from the public and judicial review.
To exploit forced labor of the prisoner population.
This purpose grew out of a labor shortage.

The majority of those who perished (die) during the Holocaust were

What happened to people when they arrived concentration camps?


As soon as the people approached at the concentration camp,
they were separated according to their gender and age group
and were given prisoner numbers. From this point onwards,
they would typically only be referred to by this number rather than their names.
Their numbers were rather stamped into their hands or stitched on the given clothes.
The given clothes were only striped pajamas with black shoes.
The prisoners (Jews) were then also told to shave their heads
and start working on the assigned job.

The First Concentration Camp


Nazi Germany's first concentration camp opened on March 22, 1933.
Communists, social democrats, and other dissidents were among the
political opponents of Hitler who were supposed to be interned in the camp.
Prisoners were brought to the camps in a variety of ways, most frequently by rail,
but occasionally by truck and, in cases when the camps were near by, on foot as well.
